Using NMR spectroscopy to study the adsorption of mAbs at hydrophobic interfaces and the effects of the addition of surfactants on the mAbs.

Research Proposal

Biopharmaceuticals, like mAbs, are becoming the drug of choice for various illness like cancer. Studies are currently being conducted on using mAbs to treat Covid-19. Monoclonal antibodies are a type of amphiphilic protein. Due to this property, the protein can undergo conformational changes at hydrophobic interfaces like air/water or oil water/interfaces. These interfaces are present when drugs are administered using IV bags or syringes. Studies have been conducted using methods like UV spectroscopy, Nuclear reflection and CD; however, these methods aren't able to provide detailed information on the changes in the conformation. We would like to use 1D and 2D NMR spectroscopy to study the structure of the mAbs in the bulk, and at a silicone oil interface. We then plan to add a surfactant, to see if that will resolve the issue of adsorption. We also plan to chemically denature mAbs to better compare the data.

Research Updates

I am in Dr. Hadi Mohammadigoshki's Lab at the FAMU-FSU College of Biological Sciences. This is the link to the groups research website. We have worked with Dr. Brian Washburn and Dr. Carl Whittington at the FSU College of Biological Sciences to generate and purify the mAbs. We have begun some NMR experiments of the purified mAbs at the NHMFL using the 800MHz NMR spectrometer. We have run experiments on a bulk sample of the mAbs. We have also performed experiments on mAbs at a hydrophobic interface using silicone oil. Using a double pulsed field gradient spin echo (DPFGSE), we plan to locate the interface.
